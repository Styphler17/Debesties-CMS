Debesties writes in a clear, direct, service-journalism style that mixes newsroom structure with reader-friendly explanations. The language is mostly plain and accessible, and it often uses short paragraphs and straightforward topic sentences to move quickly through a subject.

Across the samples, the writing frequently speaks to the reader using second-person (“you”) and practical guidance (“This guide shows…,” “To make things easier…”). The tone stays confident and explanatory, using specific examples, named entities (agencies, places, platforms), and occasional numbers to make claims feel concrete.

Headings are functional and descriptive, often phrased as questions or “How to” statements. The articles favor scannable formatting—lists, bullets, and section dividers—so readers can skim and still take away the main points.
